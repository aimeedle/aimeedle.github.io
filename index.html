Clothing Sales & COVID-19

by Aimee Le

Mens, womens, and family clothing sales

How these retail stores are doing during the pandemic

Athleta

A popular yoga apparel brand, highly compared to Lululemon, is also known for selling workout gear like sports bras and leggings for women. The Gap labeled company reported net sales were down 8%, store sales were down 50%, with online sales up 49% for the first quarter of 2020.

Source: Gap Inc.

Indochino

A Vancouver-based "made-to-measure" mens wear brand were forced to close 52 of its retail stores in the US and transfer business online, where it initially grew. The compnay have used e-commerce strategies such as re-creating a showroom experience online and allowing customers to book a 30-minute virtual appointment with a stylist, helping them design a suit or sports jacket.

Source: Drew Green, CEO of Indochino via WWD

Old Navy

A value-oriented apparel brand for families reported net sales were down 42%, store sales were down 60%, with online sales up 20% for the first quarter of 2020. The lower-priced brand owned by Gap Inc. faced challenges before the pandemic but still remained the company's strongest brand with $7.3 billion in net sales in 2019. "Since the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic, Old Navy has seen an increase in its digital business."

Source: Gap Inc. and Business Off Fashion
